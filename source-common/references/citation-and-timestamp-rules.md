# Citation and Timestamp Rules

## Capture

For each useful source, preserve when available:

- title
- source/org/author
- URL
- publish/update time
- retrieval time for live/current pages
- source type and quality tier
- language when not English
- key claims and caveats

## Cite

- Cite claim clusters, not every sentence.
- Use labelled Markdown links in chat; avoid raw URLs unless asked.
- Include concrete dates for current, market, legal, policy, release, or news claims.
- If a source lacks a timestamp, say so when freshness matters.
- Respect quote limits; summarize rather than copying long passages.

## Dedupe

Dedupe by canonical URL, title/event/entity/date, arXiv ID, repo full name, ticker/coin/date, product name, or social status ID.

## Multilingual handling

Translate non-English titles, claims, and summaries into English by default. Preserve original names, product terms, paper titles, and phrases when translation may lose meaning. Mark uncertain translation briefly instead of pretending certainty.
