# Research Contract

This is the mandatory spine for source-gathering skills.

## Lifecycle

1. State the question, entity, time window, and decision/use case.
2. Pick one source lane or a small source plan; do not browse broadly by default.
3. Gather the minimum viable source set for the effort tier.
4. Rank source quality before trusting claims.
5. Cross-check important, current, financial, legal, medical, safety, or controversial claims.
6. Separate fact, source claim, social signal, and inference.
7. Cite factual claim clusters with links and dates/timestamps when available.
8. Stop when evidence is sufficient, blocked, or diminishing returns are clear.
9. Report missing evidence and uncertainty instead of filling gaps with vibes.

## Effort tiers

- Quick: enough high-quality sources for a short answer; usually 1 lane.
- Standard: normal source scan; usually 1-3 lanes and contradiction checks where useful.
- Deep: multiple source classes, counter-evidence, stronger citations, and an evidence map.
- Watchlist: only new/material changes since last known state.

Quality beats count. Do not pad a brief with weak sources when primary sources answer the question.

## Scheduled/chat brief rule

Collectors gather notes; they do not final-render scheduled/chat briefs. For any cron-style, Telegram-readable, daily/weekly scan, or named recurring brief, hand normalized notes to `source-briefing` and use `chat-brief-style.md` card structure. Do not output a flat bullet wall from a collector.

## Stop conditions

Stop or mark blocked when:

- the user question is too ambiguous to choose source classes safely
- sources are too stale for a current claim
- critical claims lack primary/current corroboration
- the next step requires login/cookies/private data/external writes
- the marginal sources are repeating the same claims without new evidence
