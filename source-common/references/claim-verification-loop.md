# Claim Verification Loop

Use this whenever a claim affects the answer.

## Loop

1. Extract the claim precisely.
2. Identify who is making it and whether they are primary, secondary, or signal.
3. Find corroboration or contradiction from a stronger/different source class.
4. Mark the claim as fact, reported claim, inference, signal, or unverified.
5. Keep the claim only if it is useful and properly labelled.

## Contradictions

When sources conflict:

- prefer primary/current sources for direct facts
- preserve the disagreement instead of averaging it away
- say which source is newer or more direct
- downgrade confidence when evidence is thin or circular

## Counter-evidence triggers

Seek counter-evidence when the user asks for evaluation/comparison/risk, when claims are high-stakes or controversial, when a dominant narrative may be biased, or when sources mostly repeat social/aggregator claims.
