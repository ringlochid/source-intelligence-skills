# Source Research

Use for papers, research updates, arXiv scans, literature maps, and research trend discovery.

Read first:
- `../source-common/references/safety-boundaries.md`
- `../source-common/references/research-contract.md`
- `../source-common/references/stop-rules.md`
- `../source-common/references/source-quality-ladder.md`
- `../source-common/references/citation-and-timestamp-rules.md`
- `../source-common/references/claim-verification-loop.md`

Source hints: use arXiv plus Semantic Scholar/OpenAlex, OpenReview/conference pages, Papers with Code, and GitHub when relevant.

Workflow:
1. Search by query/category/author/date window.
2. Preserve title, authors, paper ID, submitted/updated date, URL, abstract, venue/categories.
3. Separate paper claims from your interpretation.
4. For important/ambiguous claims, inspect method/results or PDF figures before strong conclusions.
5. Cluster trends by problem/method/benchmark/dataset/lab/toolchain.
6. Send notes to `source-briefing`.

Output:
- research scope
- paper/source notes
- clusters/signals
- caveats/contradictions
- missing evidence
- citation/source link
